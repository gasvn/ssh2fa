How it works

Type your 2FA once. Then just ssh.

A small background helper handles the tedious part, so your terminal stays exactly the way you like it.

1

Import your hosts

SSH2FA reads your ~/.ssh/config — enable hosts in one click, or add one and scan its 2FA QR. Credentials live in the macOS Keychain.

2

It stays warm

The daemon keeps a connection alive per host and auto-recovers it after sleep, network changes, and reboots.

3

Connect instantly

Run ssh <alias> in your own terminal — it reuses the warm connection. SSH2FA sets that up for you; you edit nothing.

TOUCH ID

Your fingerprint, back in the loop.

  • Biometric prompt before credentials are revealed

  • Entirely optional — off by default, on when you want it

Security, stated plainly

SSH2FA stores your SSH password and TOTP secret in the macOS Keychain and submits the second factor for you. That convenience means your "second factor" no longer protects you against someone with access to your unlocked Mac — so you can turn on an optional Touch ID lock to require your fingerprint before any credential is revealed. Read the full security model before you rely on it.
